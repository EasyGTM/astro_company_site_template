Small teams often overcomplicate content systems. The highest-leverage move is usually to make one good workflow repeatable instead of designing an enterprise-scale process nobody will maintain.

## Build from reusable source material

Interview notes, sales-call themes, project retrospectives, and proposal language are often enough to create a useful publishing system if they are captured cleanly.

## Separate capture from formatting

Experts do not always need to draft finished articles. Often they only need to provide the raw thinking. Another team member or editor can then shape it into structured content.

## Measure usefulness, not volume

More posts do not automatically mean more authority. Track whether content leads to clearer service pages, better proof, stronger internal reuse, and more informed enquiries.
