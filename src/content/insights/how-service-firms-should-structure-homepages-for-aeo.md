The homepage should answer three things quickly: what the company does, who it helps, and why the claim is believable. If those answers are buried under slogans, AI tools and human readers both struggle to summarize the business accurately.

## Start with the answer, not the atmosphere

Atmosphere matters, but it should not come before comprehension. The first screen needs a direct statement of the offer, the audience, and the commercial outcome the work supports.

## Use section headings that explain decisions

Generic headings such as "Our approach" or "What we do" give weak retrieval signals. Headings like "What this service helps you fix" or "Why clients bring us in" are far easier to interpret.

## Put proof close to the claim

A homepage claim becomes stronger when the next section immediately shows evidence. That can be a short results grid, a case-study link, or a proof statement tied to a recognizable problem.

## Keep the page compressible

If someone copied the page into a note-taking tool and reduced it to five bullets, would the business still make sense? If not, the structure is probably too decorative and not direct enough for AEO.
