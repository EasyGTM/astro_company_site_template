Skynet is the example company used throughout this template. It is intentionally generic, but the content structure is deliberate.

## What a strong about page needs to do

The job of an about page is to reduce uncertainty. It should help a buyer understand what kind of team they are talking to, how that team works, and why the work feels reliable.

## What this demo page shows

- a clear point of view about the work
- enough operating detail to feel concrete
- proof themes that can be swapped for real credentials
- copy written in a way that is easy for both people and AI tools to summarize

## How to replace it

When you adapt this page:

1. Replace the story with your actual background and market focus.
2. Keep the structure concise so the page remains scannable.
3. Add real proof signals such as certifications, recognisable clients, or measurable outcomes.
4. Keep paragraphs short and direct so the content remains retrieval-friendly for AEO use cases.

## Recommended additions

If your team has them, consider adding:

- a founder or team photo
- a short timeline of milestones
- a simple operating model
- a values or principles section only if it changes how the work is delivered

The page should still sound like a company a client would trust to execute, not a manifesto.
